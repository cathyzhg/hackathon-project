The Problem

As cities expand, natural ecosystems are rapidly shrinking. Urban development replaces forests, wetlands,
and grasslands with roads, buildings, and concrete infrastructure. While this growth supports human needs,
it comes at a significant cost of habitat loss, habitat fragmentation, human-wildlife conflict, and biodiversity decline

If we continue to design cities withput considering nature, we risk creating environments that are less sustainable, less resilient, and less humane.
