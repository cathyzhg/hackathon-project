The Problem



As cities expand, natural ecosystems are rapidly shrinking. Urban development replaces forests, wetlands, and grasslands with roads, buildings, and concrete infrastructure. While this growth supports human needs, it comes at a significant cost of:


  • Habitat loss: Wildlife loses access to food, shelter, and breeding grounds
  • Habitat fragmentation: Roads and buildings divide ecosystems into isolated patches
  • Human-wildlife conflict:h Animals forced into cities may cause accidents or be harmed
  • Biodiversity decline: hSensitive species disappear, leaving only highly adaptable animals

If we continue to design cities withput considering nature, we risk creating environments that are less sustainable, less resilient, and less humane.
